About Me

My programming journey began in 2010 (age 12), when I opened the first page of “Hello World! Computer Programming for Kids and Other Beginners,” written by Carter Sande and Warren Sande. I set there, next to a local tech guru and programmer, contemplating what programming was and why it was important. After a couple of lessons in Python and a seeming lack of interest on my part, I called it quits. Little did I know, programming would become my passion.

I wrote my first Objective-C iOS app in October 2013 using a Udemy course as my guide. Writing Objective-C seemed difficult at the time, and it wasn’t until October of the following year that I fell in love with programming via the Swift language. Every day after school I would work on this Udemy course. This led me to release my first app, a tip calculator, on the iOS app store in December 2015.

My love for programming led me to the Florida Institute of Technology, where I graduated Magma Cum Laude with a degree in Computer Science. The small class sizes and personable professors allowed me to excel in the field of programming. I was a member of the ACM and the FIT Programming Club. During my summer break going into senior year, I completed the Treehouse Front-End Web Development Techdegree, which led me to transition from iOS development to web development.

After two wonderful internships at Kaleida Health, I have accepted an offer from Qualtrics where I will be a software engineer on the Consistent Front-End team.

I believe that the key to creating a great product is bridging the gap between technology and humans. By following this philosophy, I hope to transform the way people use technology. I intend on supplementing my technical degree with an MBA in a few years in order to come full circle in pursuing my passions of technology and management.
