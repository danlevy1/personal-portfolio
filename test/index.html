About Me

Bio

My programming journey began in 2010 (age 12), when I opened the first page of “Hello World! Computer Programming for Kids and Other Beginners,” written by Carter Sande and Warren Sande. I set there, next to a local tech guru and programmer, contemplating what programming was and why I was here. After a couple of lessons and a seeming lack of interest on my part, I called it quits. Little did I know, programming would become my passion.

I wrote my first Objective-C iOS app in October 2013 using a Udemy course as my guide. Writing Objective-C seemed difficult at the time, and it wasn’t until October of the following year that I fell in love with programming via the Swift language. Every day, I raced home from school to work on my Udemy course. This led me to release my first app, a tip calculator, on the iOS app store in December 2015.

My love for programming led me to the Florida Institute of Technology, where I am currently a senior in Computer Science. The small class sizes and personable professors (and of course the weather) have allowed me to excel in the field of programming. I am a member of the ACM as well as the FIT Programming Club. In the little free time that I currently have, I work on the Treehouse Front-End Web Development Techdegree where I make awesome websites.

As this techdegree comes to a close in September and I graduate with my B.S. in Computer Science in May, I am looking for a job prospect where I get to unleash my creativity in the form of code. I believe that the key to creating a great product is bridging the gap between technology and humans. By following this philosophy, I hope to transform the way people use technology. I intend on supplementing my technical degree with an MBA in a few years in order to come full circle in pursuing my passions of technology and management.
